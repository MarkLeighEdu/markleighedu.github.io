### USD INDEX

As the middle east war goes into a 2nd week, the financial picture has taken on a new perspective. The USD started the week strong, but has not held on to the initial gains as the dollar lost ground later in the week, and then poor job numbers on Friday added to the retracement. Note current resistance area has served as previous support and resistance which adds relevance to the level. As a general rule, war will increase inflation risks globally. Resistance at 99.63 with support at 97.69. 

![Image](https://markleighedu.github.io/img/Mar-2026/08-Mar-2026/usdindex.jpg)

### S&P 500

Stock markets were sharply down this week, with the S&P losing 2.02%. Note lower highs and lower lows extending the downtrend. Interestingly we see the USD and equities both moving in the same direction (lower) as opposed to the traditional inverse relationship between the two. The volatility saw sharp bounces, with the overall pattern reflecting early sell-offs (Asian and European sessions), with the market recovering and recouping losses through the US session. Resistance at 6903 with support at 6718.

![Image](https://markleighedu.github.io/img/Mar-2026/08-Mar-2026/sp500.jpg)

### GOLD

Clearly, and somewhat surprisingly, the metals safe have status was questionable through the first week of the middle east war. A sharp spike to start the week, without much follow through. While sellers have not followed through with the brief sell-off, buyers have not jumped in from a value prospective with prices sitting $500 lower than record high $5600 (January 29 2026). The $5100 area has played out as a pivotal level. Resistance at $5420 with support at $4992.

![Image](https://markleighedu.github.io/img/Mar-2026/08-Mar-2026/gold.jpg)

### BRENT OIL

As the war goes into it's 2nd week, the oil price continues to climb exponentially. With all the gulf states engaged and the shipping routes (Straits of Hormuz) threatened, accelerated bombing of oil facilities throughout the region escalating, supply concerns have dictated the soaring prices. The $100 oil price is the next target. The last time this commodity reached current levels was September 2023. Note the descending trendline broken to the upside. Resistance at $94.44 with support at $75.83.

![Image](https://markleighedu.github.io/img/Mar-2026/08-Mar-2026/brentoil.jpg)

### BITCOIN

The green weekly candlestick breaks the 6 consecutive red/down candles on the weekly chart. However, despite the technicals, the lack of demand for BTC continues. A single day break above $70K could not be maintained as sellers took control, bringing prices down to the now comfortable $65K - $68K range. Note the $65K support has been guarded all through the week, so far. Should downward pressure continue, the $60K low from early February looks to be the downside target. Once again, the cryptocoin has not been seen as a safe haven asset as the geopolitical conflict continues. Resistance at $74K with support at $65K.
